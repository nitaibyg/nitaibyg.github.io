The Urban Basin, Bnei Brak

3rd year, Bezalel Academy of Arts and Design, Department of Architecture. 2026

Framed within the five stages of grief, the project explores the deteriorating symbiosis between urban development and natural waterways within the Yarkon river basin. Focusing on Bnei Brak, Israel’s densest city, where a 90% impermeable surface cover has erased historic drainage channels. The urban fabric currently acts as a continuous flood catalyst during extreme climate events. The project shifts from a stance of “confrontation” to “acceptance” by treating the city itself as an active watershed. By strategically peeling back the asphalt at critical topographic lows, such as the Amiel Street residential block and the northern BBC (Bnei Brak Business Center) district. The intervention restores natural infiltration, biological filtration, and seasonal retention pools. Elevated housing units and adaptable public topographies integrate water management into daily life, converting recurring climate hazards into dynamic, much needed public parks and shared urban spaces.
