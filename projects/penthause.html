THE PENTHAUSE, Karavan Block, Tel Aviv

2nd year, Bezalel Department of Architecture, 2024/25
Instructed by Arch. Alon Sarig, assisted by Arch. Yssaf Ochana

Situated within a dense block in central Tel Aviv, the Caravan Block project re-examines the rooftop layer, focusing on the informal typology of the improvised “penthouse”. Rather than a space of luxury, these rooftops host small, low-cost, and makeshift residential adaptations. Investigating this vernacular condition, the project proposes a temporary co-living system that scales up the concept of affordable housing. Superimposed onto existing roofs, the lightweight framework provides a shared infrastructure where residents can organically construct flexible, inexpensive units. The intervention re-establishes vertical circulation from the street, transforming privatized roofs into communal, productive spaces that challenge conventional residential models.
